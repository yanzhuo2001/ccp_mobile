Double Chocolate Cookies

Origin: Michigan Source: Family Recipe Category: Dessert

My daughter learned to make these cookies at a baking camp at Zingermanns and has tweaked the recipe to fit the taste buds of her siblings. They are extremely sugary so the salt helps to balance it. Note, these cookies are best eaten very quickly.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Unsalted butter
  • Granulated Sugar
  • Packed light or dark brown sugar
  • Large egg
  • Pure vanilla extract
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chunks (melted)
  • All-purpose flour
  • Natural unsweetened cocoa powder
  • Baking soda
  • Salt
  • Semi-sweet chocolate chunks

Recipe Steps

  1. In a mixing bowl cream together the butter, granulated sugar, and brown sugar
  2. Add the egg and vanilla extract and beat well
  3. Add the melted chocolate
  4. In a separate bowl combine the flour, baking soda, cocoa powder and salt
  5. Combine the wet and dry ingredients
  6. Add the unmelted chocolate chunks.
  7. Form 15 cookies and place on a baking sheet.
  8. Cook for 12 to 13 minutes at 350 degrees.

Chicken Curry

Origin: Japan Source: Family Recipe Category: Main Dish

Unlike Indian or Thai curry, Japanese curry is more savory than spicy. It's an exemplary comfort food and one of the most-loved home-cooked dishes in Japan.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Skinless, boneless chicken breast
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 pinch salt and ground black pepper
  • Water
  • 1 package curry sauce mix
  • 1 can peas
  • 5 new potatoes, halved
  • 1 package sliced cremini mushrooms
  • 2 medium carrots, chopped
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cups jasmine rice

Recipe Steps

  1. Place chicken in a large, deep skillet. Drizzle with olive oil and season with salt and pepper on both sides. Add 1/3 cup water to the pan, cover, and cook over medium heat until no longer pink in the center and the juices run clear, about 20 minutes.
  2. While chicken is cooking, pour 2 cups water into a microwave-safe bowl. Break curry sauce mix into pieces and add to water. Heat in the microwave on high for 3 1/2 minutes; remove and stir until sauce mix has completely dissolved.
  3. Remove chicken from the skillet. Cut into cubes, return to the skillet, and pour curry sauce over top. Stir in peas, potatoes, mushrooms, carrots, and onion. Bring to a boil, then lower heat and simmer until vegetables are tender, about 30 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, bring remaining water and rice to a boil in a saucepan. Reduce heat to medium-low, cover, and simmer until rice is tender and water has been absorbed, 20 to 25 minutes.
  5. Scoop rice into bowls and serve curry on top.

Chinese Jiaozi

Origin: China Source: Family Recipe Category: Staple Food

Chinese dumplings (Jiaozi, 饺子) are stuffed parcels made of unleavened dough and savoury fillings consisting of minced ingredients like meat, egg, tofu, or vegetables. They can be boiled, pan-fried or steamed.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Flour
  • Water
  • Pork
  • Cabbage

Recipe Steps

  1. Mix flour and water to form a dough. Rest for 30 minutes.
  2. Mix minced pork with finely chopped cabbage, season with salt and pepper.
  3. Roll out dough and cut into circles. Place filling in the center of each circle.
  4. Fold and seal edges to form dumplings.
  5. Boil, steam, or pan-fry the dumplings as preferred.
  6. Serve with a dipping sauce of soy sauce, vinegar, and chili oil.

Italian Carbonara

Origin: Italy Source: Family Recipe Category: Main Dish

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with egg, hard cheese, pancetta, and pepper. It's rich and creamy without using cream.

Recipe Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Pancetta or bacon
  • Grated Pecorino Romano or Parmesan cheese
  • Eggs
  • Black pepper
  • Salt

Recipe Steps

  1. Cook spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente.
  2. In a separate pan, cook the pancetta until crisp.
  3. Beat eggs and cheese together, adding black pepper to taste.
  4. Drain the spaghetti and mix with the hot pancetta, then add the egg mixture.
  5. Stir quickly until the eggs thicken but do not scramble.
  6. Serve immediately with extra grated cheese and black pepper.

Lemon Tart

Origin: France Source: Family Recipe Category: Dessert

A Lemon Tart is a dessert dish, a variety of tart. It has a pastry shell with a lemon flavored filling.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1/4 cup powdered sugar
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 teaspoons lemon zest
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice

Recipe Steps

  1. Mix flour, butter, and powdered sugar and press into a tart pan. Bake until lightly browned.
  2. Whisk together eggs, granulated sugar, lemon zest, and lemon juice. Pour over the baked crust.
  3. Bake until filling is set. Let cool and serve.

Beef Stroganoff

Origin: Russia Source: Family Recipe Category: Main Dish

Beef Stroganoff is a Russian dish of sautéed pieces of beef served in a sauce with sour cream.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 500g beef sirloin, sliced into strips
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 200g mushrooms, sliced
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tablespoon mustard
  • 1 cup sour cream
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Recipe Steps

  1. Sauté beef strips in olive oil until browned. Remove and set aside.
  2. In the same pan, cook onion and garlic. Add mushrooms and cook until soft.
  3. Add beef broth and mustard, bring to a simmer.
  4. Return beef to the pan, add sour cream, and heat through without boiling. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Serve with noodles or rice.

Bruschetta

Origin: Italy Source: Family Recipe Category: Snacks

Bruschetta is an Italian appetizer that consists of grilled bread rubbed with garlic and topped with olive oil and salt. Variations may include toppings of tomato, vegetables, beans, cured meat, or cheese.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 4 large slices of rustic bread
  • 2 cloves garlic, halved
  • 2 large ripe tomatoes, diced
  • Fresh basil leaves, torn
  • Olive oil
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper

Recipe Steps

  1. Grill bread slices on both sides until golden brown.
  2. Rub one side of each slice with the cut side of garlic.
  3. In a bowl, mix together tomatoes, basil, olive oil, salt, and pepper.
  4. Spoon tomato mixture onto the grilled bread. Serve immediately.

Falafel

Origin: Middle East Source: Family Recipe Category: Snacks

Falafel is a deep-fried ball or patty made from ground chickpeas, fava beans, or both. Falafel is a common dish eaten throughout the Middle East.

Recipe Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons fresh parsley, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon ground cumin
  • 1 teaspoon ground coriander
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Recipe Steps

  1. Drain and rinse the chickpeas and then grind them with onion, garlic, parsley, cumin, coriander, salt, and pepper in a food processor until blended but not pureed.
  2. Form the mixture into small balls or patties.
  3. Heat oil in a deep fryer or large pan to 350°F (175°C). Fry the falafel balls until golden brown.
  4. Drain on paper towels. Serve hot with tahini sauce or as a falafel sandwich.

Onigiri

Origin: Japan Source: Family Recipe Category: Staple Food

Onigiri, also known as Japanese rice ball, is a Japanese food made from white rice formed into triangular or cylindrical shapes and often wrapped in nori (seaweed).

Recipe Ingredients

  • 2 cups sushi rice
  • 2 1/2 cups water
  • Salt to taste
  • Nori seaweed sheets
  • Filling options: umeboshi (pickled plum), cooked salmon, tuna mayonnaise, etc.

Recipe Steps

  1. Rinse the sushi rice until the water runs clear, then cook it with the water and let it cool slightly.
  2. Wet your hands with water and salt them lightly. Take about a half cup of rice and form it into a triangular shape, creating a small well in the center for the filling.
  3. Put your choice of filling into the well, then mold more rice on top to cover it.
  4. Wrap the rice ball in a strip of nori seaweed for easy handling.
  5. Onigiri can be served immediately or packed for a meal on the go.
